AI in Fiction Assignment by Sedona Sky Zeller

Artifact's Description

I drafted this comprehensive policy on the ethical and responsible use of generative AI specifically for the Fall 2023 ENGL 101-426 class at the University of Arizona, taught by Professor Jamey Rogers. This policy outlines detailed guidelines for distinguishing between AI-assisted and original student work, emphasizing the importance of transparency and proper citation. It covers using AI for creating content like homework, essays, and reports. The policy also stipulates the necessity of explicitly citing all uses of generative AI, detailing the consequences of failure to adhere to these guidelines, including disciplinary actions in line with the University's academic integrity code.

Assignment's Goals

The project aimed to establish a clear, ethical framework for using generative AI in academic settings. The primary goal was to protect academic integrity within the class by providing clear and precise guidelines on AI usage. I designed the policy to help students understand the difference between leveraging AI as a tool and relying on it to the point of compromising their original work. It also aimed to foster a sense of responsibility and awareness among students regarding the implications of using AI in their academic work.

Why this Artifact?

I included the AI Policy in my portfolio as it demonstrates my application of Rhetorical Awareness and Conventions from English 101, showcasing my skills in crafting clear, effective policy documents with an emphasis on ethical considerations, such as the explicit citation of AI use, which highlights my understanding of academic integrity and technology's role in education.
