Frequently Asked Questions For English 101


Welcome to the FAQ section of "Engineering Words: My Journey Through English 101." Here, you'll find answers and insights that reflect my experiences in academic writing, tailored for future English 101 students seeking guidance and inspiration in their writing journey.


A: Engaging with projects like the AI Ethics Case Study Analysis will deepen your understanding of complex issues, such as the ethical implications of AI in education. As you explore cases, like a student inadvertently using AI, you'll enhance your problem-solving and critical-thinking abilities. This process aligns with the course's Rhetorical Awareness and Critical Thinking goals. For instance, you might examine scenarios where a professor must choose between upholding academic integrity or using the situation as a learning opportunity about AI tools' due diligence, as I highlighted in my analysis: "The professor must decide between upholding the class rules and the fabric of academic integrity regardless of the student's intent... or using the situation to help their students understand the importance of performing due diligence on the tools they use by checking to see if they use AI" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3.). This experience will challenge you and prepare you to apply these critical analytical skills to real-world situations in your future writing tasks.

A: Reflecting on a Case Study Analysis in English 101, such as "A Case of Unintentional AI Use," is integral to developing your critical thinking and composition skills. In my experience, this reflection process allows you to revisit and reevaluate your initial analysis, providing a deeper understanding of the complexities involved. As I noted, "analyzing the case study from multiple perspectives... I realize there is no 'one size fits all' solution" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2). This kind of introspection helps you understand the multifaceted nature of ethical dilemmas, particularly in the context of AI and education, enhancing your ability to articulate well-reasoned arguments and solutions in your writing. Such reflective practices are crucial for honing your ability to compose thoughtfully and engage with complex topics critically, aligning with the goals of English 101​​.

A: Engaging with themes in fiction, such as AI in fiction, as depicted in the movie Finch, aligns with the English 101 course goal of Revision, particularly in enhancing critical thinking skills. This analysis assignment required revisiting and reinterpreting our understanding of AI, pushing us to revise our initial perceptions. In Finch, we see a transition in the role of AI from a tool to a sentient companion, which necessitates a revised view of AI's ethical treatment. As I observed in my analysis, "Finch becomes frustrated with Jeff... lashing out at him and saying he is just a machine... [but] Finch later realizes his error" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3). This narrative arc illustrates the process of Revision, not just in writing but in our cognitive approach to complex subjects like AI. Analyzing such themes in fiction helps you develop the ability to think critically about evolving concepts, revise your viewpoints, and apply these skills to your writing and broader academic work.

A: Drafting an AI Policy in English 101 demonstrates the practical application of Rhetorical Awareness and Conventions course goals. This assignment engages you in applying specific writing conventions to a policy document, which is crucial for clear and effective communication. As the policy outlines, "Students must explicitly cite all uses of generative AI" Drafting an (Zeller, 2023, p. 2). This task emphasizes the need for precision and ethical considerations in academic writing. It challenges you to consider the audience, purpose, and context of your writing, reflecting the course's focus on understanding how these elements impact research strategies, organization, and language usage. Engaging in this task will enhance your ability to think critically and solve problems, applying your understanding of genre and rhetorical situations to a real-world context.

A: In English 101, good writing is as much about reflection on the writing process as it is about the final product. Through assignments like the AI Policy Reflection assignment, you'll gain insights into your writing journey, discovering the importance of balancing diverse perspectives and ethical considerations. As I experienced, reflecting on the process "helped me grow as a critical thinker, practicing objectively looking at opposing views with little to no bias" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3). You'll learn to appreciate the research phase, understand different aspects of writing, such as policy drafting, and grapple with the nuances of complex issues, like AI ethics. Moreover, this reflective approach will enhance your understanding of the complexities of writing and the ethical implications of your work, fostering a deeper comprehension of how your values and circumstances influence your writing choices​.

A: In the Digital Literacy Narrative project, you'll craft a digital story exploring your personal experiences, similar to my own with "Beyond Doubt," which focuses on overcoming imposter syndrome. This endeavor challenges you to integrate various media formats, enhancing your storytelling skills and audience engagement. For instance, in my project, I shared how "My experiences at the school's science and engineering fair exemplified these feelings. Reluctantly I participated and invested myself in a project about bioluminescent organisms" (Zeller, 2023, Beyond Doubt [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HbiuYfBx9JI). This narrative improves your storytelling capabilities and demonstrates your literacy in a specific subject area, illustrating how you navigate and express complex topics beyond traditional reading and writing. It's an exercise that fosters emotional and intellectual growth, aiding your continuous development in academic and personal contexts.

A: In the Digital Literacy Narrative Reflection assignment, you will engage in a critical assessment of your writing approach, identifying both strengths and areas for improvement, much like I did. This reflection, integral to our course, aims to deepen your understanding of your writing and thinking processes. In my reflection, I uncovered the importance of being open and honest in my narrative, as shown by my realization that "A major lesson I learned during this exercise was the power of vulnerability in storytelling" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2). This insight emerged from sharing my personal experiences, where I found that embracing vulnerability made my narrative more relatable and impactful. Additionally, the assignment will teach you the value of conciseness in your script, which "helped me understand the importance of focusing on critical points in storytelling" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2), emphasizing the need to refine your narrative to its most essential elements. Finally, creating a digital narrative will also enhance your ability to effectively communicate complex and personal topics, improving your writing skills, emotional expression, and storytelling ability.
