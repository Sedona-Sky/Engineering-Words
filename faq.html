Frequently Asked Questions For English 101


Welcome to the FAQ section of "Engineering Words: My Journey Through English 101." Here, you'll find answers and insights that reflect my experiences in academic writing, tailored for future English 101 students seeking guidance and inspiration in their writing journey.


A: Engaging with projects like the AI Ethics Case Study Analysis will deepen your understanding of complex issues, such as the ethical implications of AI in education. As you explore cases, like a student inadvertently using AI, you'll enhance your problem-solving and critical-thinking abilities. This process aligns with the course's Rhetorical Awareness and Critical Thinking goals. For instance, you might examine scenarios where a "professor must decide between upholding the class rules and the fabric of academic integrity regardless of the student's intent... or using the situation to help their students understand the importance of performing due diligence on the tools they use by checking to see if they use AI" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3.). My analysis presents the real-world relevance of ethical decision-making in academic settings, emphasizing the importance of balancing rule enforcement with educational opportunities. Finally, this experience will challenge you and prepare you to apply these critical analytical skills to real-world situations in your future writing tasks.

A: Reflecting on a case study analysis in English 101, such as "A Case of Unintentional AI Use," is integral to developing your critical thinking and composition skills. In my experience, this reflection process allowed me to revisit and reevaluate my initial analysis, providing a deeper understanding of the complexities involved. As I noted, "analyzing the case study from multiple perspectives... I realize there is no 'one size fits all' solution" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3). This realization helped me understand the importance of considering various viewpoints and the complexity of ethical dilemmas, as every situation demands a tailored approach rather than a blanket solution. Such reflective practices are crucial for honing your ability to compose thoughtfully and engage with complex topics critically, aligning with the goals of English 101.

A: In English 101, analyzing fiction, like the portrayal of AI in the movie "Finch" offers a deep dive into how a text's genre influences reading and writing practices. The film depicts a significant shift in AI's role, evolving from a tool to a sentient companion. I noticed this shift when "Finch becomes frustrated with Jeff... lashing out at him and saying he is just a machine... [but] Finch later realizes his error" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3). These moments in "Finch" helped me understand the power of fiction to challenge and reshape my understanding of complex subjects such as technology and ethics. Analyzing such themes in fiction will enable you to develop your ability to think critically about evolving concepts, challenge your viewpoints, and apply these skills to your writing and broader academic work.

A: In drafting an AI Policy for our English 101 class, we actively engaged in the practical application of Rhetorical Awareness and Conventions course goals. This assignment required us to apply specific writing conventions for crafting clear, effective policy documents. For instance, as outlined in the policy I created, "Students must explicitly cite all uses of generative AI" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2). In writing this, I focused on balancing clarity and respectfulness, reflecting my growing understanding of rhetorical awareness. By considering student and teacher perspectives, I navigated the fine line between being explicit for comprehension and maintaining a tone respecting all parties involved. This approach not only enhanced my skills in critical analysis and addressing complex topics, but it also serves as a model for you, the reader, demonstrating how such tasks can deepen your understanding of writing conventions and rhetorical awareness in your works.

A: Good writing in English 101 is as much about reflection on the writing process as the final product. In assignments like the AI Policy Reflection, you'll discover the importance of balancing diverse perspectives and ethical considerations. Reflecting on my writing, I realized it "helped me grow as a critical thinker, practicing objectively looking at opposing views with little to no bias" (Zeller, 2023, p. 3). For example, when drafting the policy, I had to consider various perspectives on AI use in academia, ranging from very strict to more permissive, synthesizing these perspectives to form an unbiased policy. This process of reflecting and synthesizing diverse viewpoints, as you'll experience in your writing journey, not only deepens your understanding of complex and ethical writing but also illustrates the essence of good writing in English 101 – the ability to articulate informed, balanced, and unbiased perspectives shaped by thorough research and reflection.

A: In the Digital Literacy Narrative project, you will create a digital story as a video about how you developed literacy in a particular area, demonstrating that literacy extends beyond reading and writing. The video will incorporate multimedia elements like audio, images, transitions, and animated graphics. This multimedia format is pivotal in enhancing storytelling skills and audience engagement. In my video "Beyond Doubt," I focused on overcoming imposter syndrome. For instance, I detailed, "My experiences at the school's science and engineering fair exemplified these feelings. Reluctantly, I participated and invested myself in a project about bioluminescent organisms" (Zeller, 2023, Beyond Doubt [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HbiuYfBx9JI). This part of my story highlights my journey in grappling with self-doubt and fear of failure, illustrating how literacy encompasses understanding and conveying complex concepts and personal experiences. As you embark on your Digital Literacy Narrative project, you'll find that it enhances your storytelling abilities, deepens your understanding and articulation of complex subjects, and leads to discovering how your literacy has broader societal implications.

A: In the Digital Literacy Narrative Reflection assignment, you will engage in a critical assessment of your writing approach, identifying both strengths and areas for improvement, much like I did. This reflection, integral to our course, aims to deepen your understanding of your writing and thinking processes. In my reflection, I uncovered the importance of being open and honest in my narrative, as shown by my realization that "A major lesson I learned during this exercise was the power of vulnerability in storytelling" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2). This insight emerged from sharing my personal experiences, where I found that embracing vulnerability made my narrative more relatable and impactful. Additionally, the assignment will teach you the value of conciseness in your script, which "helped me understand the importance of focusing on critical points in storytelling" (Zeller, 2023, p. 2), emphasizing the need to refine your narrative to its most essential elements. Finally, creating a digital narrative will also enhance your ability to effectively communicate complex and personal topics, improving your writing skills, emotional expression, and storytelling ability.
